Defining Technical Communication

Some people say that technical communication should not be defined because it would exclude people in the grey area of technical communication who are working on something innovative and useful. However, I believe the lack of definition about what technical communication is leads to the lack of knowledge about the field and impacts the number of students in the major at Iowa State. Creating a definition for technical communication is difficult because it is a broad field and many people use it differently. The Society for Technical Communication defines it as:

  • Communicating about technical or specialized topics, such as computer applications, medical procedures, or environmental regulations.
  • Communicating by using technology, such as web pages, help files, or social media sites.
  • Providing instructions about how to do something, regardless of how technical the task is or even if technology is used to create or distribute that communication.
In my survey, I wanted to see how current technical communication students view and define technical communication. My goal for this section of the survey was to potentially define technical communication, but more importantly define it in how it is used specifically at Iowa State. I believe this would help potential students decide if it is a good fit for them.

Radar Chart

In the survey, the students were asked two questions: how they defined technical communication and what words came to mind when they thought about technical communication. Based on the results, six categories - Writing/Editing, Design, Media, STEM, Rhetoric, and Documentation - were created. Extraneous words, like "the", and adjectives, like "concise" were taken out of the definitions. The radar chart documents the number of definitions that had one (or several) words that related to any of the six categories. The chart does the same of the number of words that came to mind. Below are examples of words that fir into each catergory:

Writing & Editing - writing, wordsmithing, editing
Design - arrangement, digital design, visual
Media - websites, videos
STEM - technology, science, STEM
Rhetoric - audience specific, ethos
Documentation - charts, reports, manuals

Student Definitions

● The ability to understand complex topics quickly and effectively to then communicate out important information to specific audiences.

● Writing about procedure and guidelines.

● Communication that meant for practical means like how to do things.

● A profession that communicates complicated/technical information at the right level and in the right media for an audience. Includes information architecture, document design, and editing.

● Communicating technology and technological processes, as well as, constructing visual and textual forms of communication using technology.

● Study or broad communications with an emphasis on clearly portraying a message in an understandable and simple way.

● Engaging in forms of communication about or for a technical purpose or audience, including documents such as instruction manuals, cookbooks, and business and statistical documents.

● Concise and accurate communication catered to a specific audience done through writing, video, imagery, websites, or other forms of media.

● Communication that is specific to a domain or uses technological mediums.

● The art/act/process of conveying ideas and data through digital mediums.
